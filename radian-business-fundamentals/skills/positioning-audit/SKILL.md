# positioning-audit

## When to use

- Symptoms surface: reps describe the product differently, buyers say "I don't get what you do", competing on features instead of category, getting price-shopped
- Pre-relaunch / re-branding
- Category move or pivot
- New CEO / VP Marketing taking inventory

## Core principle

**Weak positioning is the #1 root cause of slow growth in B2B** (April Dunford / Forrester). Strong positioning moves win rate + deal size + cycle time simultaneously — the highest-leverage strategic lever. Most companies position by accident, then wonder why marketing/sales misalign.

## Audit framework (April Dunford "Obviously Awesome")

For each company, work through:

1. **Competitive alternatives** — what would the customer use if you didn't exist (often "do nothing" or a spreadsheet, not the obvious competitor)
2. **Unique attributes** — what you have that the alternatives don't (capabilities, not features)
3. **Value (and proof)** — what those attributes do for the customer (turn attributes into outcomes)
4. **Best-fit customers** — who cares most about that value
5. **Market category** — what market you're in (the most leveraged choice; often the differentiator)
6. **Optional: trends** — make the category feel current

## Workflow

1. Interview internal stakeholders (CEO, head of sales, head of marketing, top reps)
2. Interview 5-10 customers (recent won, churned, lost-to-competitor)
3. Pull current positioning artifacts (website, sales deck, pitch deck, demo)
4. Diagnose alignment / drift:
   - Where do internal answers diverge?
   - Where does customer reality diverge from internal narrative?
5. Run the 6-step framework
6. Produce the positioning statement + sales narrative
7. Validate with sales (does it sell?) and customers (do they recognize themselves?)

## Output format

Final document:

```markdown
*Positioning Audit — [Company]*

*Current positioning (as found):*
[Verbatim from website + pitch deck + sales]

*Where it breaks:*
- Internal disagreement: ...
- Customer reality mismatch: ...
- Competitive collision: ...

*Recommended positioning:*

**Competitive alternatives:** [what customers would use otherwise]
**Unique attributes:** [capabilities]
**Value:** [outcomes for the customer]
**Best-fit customers:** [target segment]
**Market category:** [chosen category]

*Positioning statement (one paragraph):*
For [best-fit customer] who [pain], [company] is a [category] that [unique outcome]. Unlike [alternative], we [differentiator].

*Sales narrative outline:*
1. [problem framing]
2. [why now]
3. [our approach]
4. [proof]
5. [outcome story]

*Implementation plan:*
- Sales deck rewrite: [scope]
- Website rewrite: [scope]
- Sales enablement: [training plan]
- Validation: [customer panel + sales-lift tracking]
```

## Connectors

- Customer interview transcripts (Gong / Fireflies)
- Sales materials access
- CRM (win/loss patterns)
- Slack (delivery)

## Gotchas

- This is NOT automated — it requires actual interviews and judgment
- Sell as advisory engagement, not productized — higher price, lower volume
- Validate with customers, not internal opinion — internal alignment can be illusion
- Don't reposition annually — it's expensive and confusing; do it when actually needed
