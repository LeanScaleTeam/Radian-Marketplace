# gtm-org-design

## When to use

- Pre-fundraise (hiring plan needs to defend)
- After a scale jump (10 → 25 people, motion is breaking)
- Existing org has structural friction (overlapping roles, missing handoffs)
- New GTM leader stepping in
- Considering a Head of X hire — is it really the next role?

## Core principle

**30%+ of B2B sales orgs have structural inefficiencies depressing productivity 10-25%** (Bridge Group). Top-quartile companies redesign GTM org every 18-24 months. Most orgs design by accident — new role per pain point — without revisiting the whole.

## Framework

1. **Define the GTM motion(s)** — SLG, PLG, channel, hybrid; what's the actual flow from lead to renewal
2. **Map the math** — pipeline math by stage, capacity per role, conversion rates
3. **Identify the bottleneck** — where does the motion break / where is capacity short
4. **Score role efficacy** — for each existing role, is it adding leverage or friction
5. **Propose the redesign** — roles, reporting, hiring sequence, comp model
6. **Sequence the change** — what order, what risks, what KPIs to watch

## Common patterns to evaluate

- **SDR vs BDR vs AE split** — is it earning its overhead, or just adding handoff friction
- **Outbound vs Inbound rep specialization** — same reps doing both = both done poorly
- **AE vs SE vs Solutions Consultant** — technical pre-sales support model
- **CS vs Account Manager vs Renewal Manager** — who owns expansion, renewal, retention
- **RevOps positioning** — under sales, marketing, CFO, or standalone
- **GTM Engineer / Clay role** — modern signal-driven role, 100+ net-new postings/month, 280+ active 2026 roles (Lovable, Cursor, Webflow, Notion adopting). Worth planning for.

## Workflow

1. Interview the GTM leader, plus 2 reps in each role
2. Pull the math:
   - Pipeline math (leads → MQL → SQL → opp → close)
   - Per-rep productivity (quota attainment by role)
   - Cycle time + win rate by motion
   - CAC + payback
3. Identify the bottleneck (where in the funnel are we capacity-constrained or motion-broken)
4. Run the redesign:
   - Roles needed
   - Specializations (yes/no, where)
   - Reporting structure
   - Comp plans aligned to motion
5. Build the hiring plan — sequence + timing + budget
6. Define success metrics — how do we know the new structure works?

## Connectors

- CRM (productivity + funnel math)
- HR system (current headcount + comp)
- Finance (budget for hires)

## Gotchas

- Don't redesign more than every 18 months — disruption has a real cost
- Watch for "we need a Head of X" as a substitute for fixing process — sometimes the right move, often not
- Test the new structure in one segment / team first when possible
- Hiring sequence matters as much as the design — wrong order creates rolling chaos
