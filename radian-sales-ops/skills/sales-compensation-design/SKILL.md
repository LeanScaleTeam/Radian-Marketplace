# sales-compensation-design

## When to use

- Annual comp plan refresh
- Strategic mix shift (more expansion needed, more new biz, etc.)
- Reps optimizing the wrong things (new biz instead of expansion, etc.)
- Comp disputes increasing (sign of plan complexity)
- Pre-fundraise (comp is a quality signal — board reviews these)

## Core principle

**Comp plan misalignment is the #1 cited reason for sales performance issues** (WorldatWork). Plans paying >70% on one metric drive narrow behavior. 60% of comp plans haven't been substantially redesigned in 3+ years.

## Plan design principles

1. **Pay for what you want to maximize** — if expansion matters, comp expansion
2. **Pay heavily on the primary metric, lightly on secondary** — not equally
3. **Keep it simple** — if reps can't explain how they're paid, the plan failed
4. **OTE that matches market** — undermarket = recruiting + retention problem
5. **Accelerators for outperformance** — not as a substitute for clear primary comp
6. **Decelerators / clawbacks for non-payment** — protect the company
7. **Comp plan ≈ strategy in dollar form** — they should match

## Common plan structures

| Role | Typical mix |
|---|---|
| **SDR / BDR** | 70/30 base/variable; variable on meetings booked + opps generated |
| **AE (new biz)** | 50/50; variable on bookings; accelerators above quota |
| **AM (expansion)** | 60/40; variable on expansion + retention; renewal at target rate |
| **CSM (with comp)** | 80/20 or 90/10; variable on retention + expansion + NPS |
| **Sales manager** | 60/40-70/30; team quota + individual MBO |

## Workflow

1. Pull current plan + historicals:
   - Pay distribution (W2 distribution by rep)
   - Plan complexity score (# of components, # of accelerators)
   - Behavior signals (what reps optimize)
2. Diagnose:
   - Where is the plan driving wrong behavior?
   - Where is it not paying enough for the right behavior?
   - Where is it too complex?
3. Strategy alignment check:
   - What does the company need more of?
   - Does comp pay for it?
4. Redesign:
   - Primary metric clearly weighted
   - Accelerators sensibly placed
   - Decelerators / clawbacks where needed
   - OTE benchmarked to market
5. Model the change:
   - Expected impact on top quartile / middle / bottom reps
   - Total comp expense vs. revenue
6. Communicate transparently
7. Run for full year before revising

## Output format

```markdown
*Comp Plan Design — [Role / Period]*

*Current plan:*
- Base / Variable: [X / Y]
- Components: [list]
- OTE: $Z
- Complexity score: [1-5]

*Diagnosis:*
- [behavior issue 1 driven by current plan]
- [behavior issue 2]
- [complexity issues]

*Proposed plan:*

**Structure:**
- Base / Variable: [X / Y]
- OTE: $Z

**Components:**
| Component | Weight | Calculation |
| Bookings | 70% | $ × N% commission |
| Expansion | 20% | $ × M% commission |
| MBO (CRM hygiene, coaching) | 10% | Manager rating |

**Accelerators:**
- 100-150% of quota: 1.5x commission rate
- 150%+: 2x commission rate

**Decelerators / clawbacks:**
- Churn within 90 days: 100% claw back
- Churn 90-180 days: 50% claw back

*Modeled impact:*
| Rep type | Old comp | New comp | Delta |
| Top quartile | $X | $Y | +$Z |
| Middle | $A | $B | $C |
| Bottom | $D | $E | $F |

*Rollout plan:*
- Communicate: [date]
- Effective: [date]
- 90-day check-in: [date]
```

## Connectors

- HR / payroll (current comp data)
- CRM (bookings + behavior data)
- Finance (comp budget)
- Optional: comp platform (Spiff / CaptivateIQ / QuotaPath)

## Gotchas

- Don't over-engineer — every additional component dilutes signal
- Comp plan changes that lower top reps' earnings = exodus risk. Grandfather where needed.
- Test math against historicals before rolling out
- Reps will figure out the loopholes faster than you — pressure-test for gaming
