# content-distribution

## When to use

- Every time a new content piece is published (auto-trigger)
- Weekly content distribution audit (any pieces undistributed?)
- After a hero piece publishes (kick off full orchestration)
- Stale traffic on evergreen content (re-distribution opportunity)

## Core principle

**80% of content effort typically goes to production; 20% to distribution.** Best-in-class teams flip this. Repurposing increases content ROI by 3-5x (Buffer / ConvertKit). A piece reaches <10% of its potential audience without intentional distribution.

## Distribution channels

| Channel | Use |
|---|---|
| **Email newsletter** | Native distribution to opted-in audience |
| **LinkedIn personal posts** (employees) | Highest organic reach in B2B |
| **LinkedIn company page** | Lower organic, but signals brand |
| **Twitter / X** | If audience is there |
| **Reddit (relevant subreddits)** | Authentic engagement, careful tone |
| **Slack communities / Circle / Discord** | High-fit audiences |
| **Internal slack channels** | Sales pulls into deals |
| **Sales / CS as distribution arm** | Reps share with prospects |
| **Paid amplification** (LinkedIn / Meta) | Boost what's already winning organically |
| **Partner co-marketing** | Reciprocal distribution |
| **PR / podcast outreach** | For hero pieces |

## Repurposing matrix

One long-form piece → many derivatives:

| Original | Derivatives |
|---|---|
| 2,000-word blog post | LinkedIn carousel, Twitter thread, 60-sec video, podcast episode, newsletter feature, 3 LinkedIn posts, slide deck |
| Original research | Webinar, PR pitch, sales deck, infographic, customer case |
| Webinar recording | Blog post, clips for social, gated lead magnet |

## Workflow

1. New content piece published
2. Auto-generate the distribution checklist:
   - Native channels (newsletter, LinkedIn company)
   - Employee advocacy assets
   - Repurposing derivatives queued
   - Sales / CS handoff with usage suggestions
3. Schedule the rollout (don't dump everything on day 1)
4. Track engagement per channel
5. Boost what's winning (paid amplification)
6. Refresh quarterly (re-distribute evergreen)

## Output format

Per publish:

```markdown
*Distribution Plan — "[Content title]" — Published [date]*

*Native channels (week 1):*
- [ ] Newsletter — schedule for [date]
- [ ] LinkedIn company page — schedule [date]
- [ ] Internal Slack — #all-company + #sales

*Employee advocacy (week 1-2):*
- LinkedIn post angle for [exec 1]: [hook]
- LinkedIn post angle for [exec 2]: [different hook]
- Twitter thread draft: [link]

*Repurposing (week 2-4):*
- [ ] LinkedIn carousel — design brief sent
- [ ] 60-sec video clip — script attached
- [ ] Newsletter feature in next 2 editions

*Sales / CS handoff (week 1):*
- [ ] #sales channel post: "Use this with prospects evaluating [X]"
- [ ] CSM channel post: "Send to customers facing [Y]"

*Paid amplification (week 3 — once organic traction confirmed):*
- LinkedIn boost: $X budget, [audience targeting]

*Refresh (90 days post-publish):*
- [ ] Re-share to LinkedIn
- [ ] Newsletter re-feature with new framing
```

## Connectors

- CMS (publish trigger)
- Newsletter platform (Beehiiv / Substack / Customer.io / Mailchimp)
- LinkedIn (employee advocacy tool: e.g. EveryoneSocial / Hootsuite Amplify)
- Slack
- Ad platforms (for paid amplification)

## Gotchas

- Distribution > production. If you can only do one well, do distribution.
- Don't dump everything on day 1 — sustained rollout > burst
- Employee advocacy is the highest-leverage B2B channel — invest in tools + training
- Track per-piece distribution coverage as a KPI (target: every piece hits N channels minimum)
