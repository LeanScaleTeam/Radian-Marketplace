# renewal-management

## When to use

- 90 days before every renewal (auto-trigger)
- Cohort review: all renewals in upcoming quarter
- Post-mortem on a slipped / lost renewal
- After a contract change / pricing change (re-prep affected renewals)

## Core principle

**30-50% of renewals are influenced by competitive alternatives.** Structured renewal motions lift retention 10-20 points vs. reactive. Renewal slippage = re-negotiation in the customer's favor.

## Renewal motion phases

| Days before renewal | Phase | Actions |
|---|---|---|
| 90 | Discovery | Health check, stakeholder mapping, value review prep |
| 75 | Value review call | Show ROI delivered, surface upsell options |
| 60 | Commercial intro | Multi-year / expansion / pricing change presented |
| 45 | Negotiation | Procurement engaged, paper terms |
| 30 | Close | Sign by this point — any past = slippage risk |
| 15 | Escalation | If unsigned, escalate to exec sponsor |

## Workflow

1. Trigger 90 days before renewal date
2. Pull account state:
   - Health score / risk signals
   - ARR / contract terms
   - Usage / adoption
   - Open opportunities / engagements
   - Stakeholder map
3. Generate the renewal prep packet:
   - Value delivered (metrics moved, outcomes achieved)
   - Risk flags
   - Upsell / multi-year recommendations
   - Suggested call agenda
4. Schedule the value review call (75 days out)
5. Track phase progression — escalate if behind schedule
6. Post-renewal: capture concessions / pricing decisions for future calibration

## Connectors

- CRM (required)
- CS tool (required)
- Product analytics
- Slack (delivery)

## Gotchas

- Procurement engagement timing — late = slip
- Multi-year offers are highest-leverage at renewal, often missed entirely
- Track renewal cycle time (90d-out start → signed) — outliers are warning signs
- Concessions track separately — surface to leadership monthly
