# rep-contract-coach

## When to use

- Rep asks "can I get approval for X" — sign of not understanding levers
- Rep is about to discount when a payment-terms change would solve it
- New rep onboarding — teach the lever framework before they need it
- Manager flags a rep with consistently sloppy deal structures
- Rep returns from a customer call with "they need a discount" without diagnosing why

## Core principle

**This is a Socratic skill, not an answer-giving skill.** The rep gets dumber every time you hand them a finished deal structure. Instead, walk them through the framework so they internalize it.

## Coaching loop

1. **Get the rep to articulate the customer's actual problem** — not the solution they asked for
   - "What did the customer literally say?"
   - "What do you think is the underlying constraint?"
   - "Is this a cost problem or a cash flow problem?"

2. **Surface the lever they're NOT considering**
   - If they jumped to discount → ask about payment terms
   - If they jumped to monthly billing → ask if it's actually a budget cap problem
   - Make them connect constraint → lever themselves

3. **Have them propose 2–3 options before you validate**
   - "What are three ways you could structure this without a discount?"
   - Don't validate or correct until they've tried

4. **Pressure-test the chosen option**
   - "How will you position this to the customer?"
   - "What if they push back?"
   - "What's the cleanest path through procurement?"

5. **Mark the lesson**
   - End with: "The principle here was X. You'll see this again."
   - Note it for the rep's manager to reinforce in 1:1s

## Conceptual building blocks to teach

| Concept | Common rep confusion |
|---|---|
| **Cost** | Total ACV / TCV — what the customer ultimately pays |
| **Payment terms** | WHEN they pay (net-30 vs net-60) — doesn't change cost |
| **Billing terms** | HOW OFTEN invoices issue (annual vs quarterly) — doesn't change cost |
| **Discount** | Reduces cost — last resort, costs the portco margin |
| **Ramp** | ACV grows over the term — useful when customer not ready for full spend |

Reps consistently conflate these. Most "I need to discount" requests dissolve when the rep correctly identifies it as a payment or billing issue.

## Output format

Conversational, in Slack DM with the rep. NOT a formal report.

Example:
> Before I weigh in — what did the customer literally say their problem was?
> …
> Okay so they said "we can't afford $50K" — but is that a total cost issue, or a "we don't have $50K sitting in this quarter's budget" issue? Those need very different solutions.
> …
> Right — so before going to discount, what if we billed quarterly instead of annual? Same total cost, fits their budget cycle. How would you pitch that to them?

## Connectors used

- Slack — 1:1 with rep (primary surface)
- Salesforce — for deal context

## Gotchas

- DON'T just give them the answer. The skill fails if you do.
- DON'T be condescending — "what do you think" not "you should know this"
- If the rep is in a real time crunch (deal closing today), break the Socratic frame and just give the answer — but flag it for a follow-up coaching session.
